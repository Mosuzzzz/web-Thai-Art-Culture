Majestic Temples

Sacred Architecture of Thailand

Wat Phra Kaew

The Temple of the Emerald Buddha (Wat Phra Kaew) is regarded as the most sacred Buddhist temple in Thailand. Located within the grounds of the Grand Palace in Bangkok, it houses the Emerald Buddha, a potent religion-political symbol and the palladium of Thai society.


The architecture is a stunning example of the Rattanakosin style, featuring intricate mosaics, golden chedis, and mythical guardians.

Wat Arun

Wat Arun Ratchawararam Ratchawaramahawihan, or the Temple of Dawn, is a Buddhist temple in Bangkok's Yai district on the Thonburi west bank of the Chao Phraya River. The temple derives its name from the Hindu god Aruna, often personified as the radiations of the rising sun.


Its central prang (spire) is encrusted with colorful porcelain and seashells, making it one of the most iconic landmarks of Bangkok.
